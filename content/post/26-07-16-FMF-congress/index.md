**Marta Núñez**, **César Díaz** and **Gabriel Bernardino** attended the 23rd World Congress in Fetal Medicine (FMF), held this year in Vienna from June 28th to July 2nd, gathering thousands of specialists in fetal-maternal medicine from around the world.

<!--more-->
<p style="text-align: justify;">
The World Congress in Fetal Medicine, organized by the Fetal Medicine Foundation, is one of the most important international meetings in the field of prenatal diagnosis and fetal-maternal care. Held annually in a different host city, this year the congress took place at the Austria Center Vienna, bringing together leading experts, researchers and clinicians to discuss the latest advances in early risk assessment, fetal imaging and precision diagnostics. It represents a key event to stay up to date with developments that directly impact clinical practice and research worldwide.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
During the congress, Marta, César and Gabriel attended several oral sessions covering topics closely related to their research, including preeclampsia prediction, fetal growth restriction, and the growing role of artificial intelligence in prenatal diagnosis. 
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Attending this type of international congress allows the team to exchange ideas with leading researchers in the field, identify emerging trends, and establish potential collaborations. It also provides valuable visibility for the project, reinforcing its position within the international fetal medicine research community.
</p>
